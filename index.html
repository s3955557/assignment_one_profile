Project Idea

The Concept

The project idea that I would like to develop is that of a crowdfunding platform to invest in esports ventures such as esports teams and athletes. I would like to innovate a way for esports teams and athletes to be able to monetise their future potential earnings from endorsement and sponsorship deals as well as earnings from esports competitions. The benefit that the platform would bring to esport teams and athletes is to gain access to funds earlier in their careers for the purpose of investing in themselves, covering costs such as gaming equipment and travel to events, as even for personal projects such as obtaining better schooling and housing. The benefit this would bring to investors looking to invest in esports teams and athletes is the opportunity to participate in a percentage of future earnings attained by the esport teams and athletes which can be highly lucrative, as well as to be part of an exciting fast-growing niche industry in the early phase of its life cycle.

Motivation

Why is this project important? Video gaming is no longer just a hobby or pastime. It is quite common now for professional gamers to compete around the world in tournaments for substantial prize money in broadcast events. Much like professional sports teams, esport organisations have been established, backed by investors, with the purpose of recruiting the best gamers from around to represent them in tournaments. The esports industry is made up of revenue from physical and digital games sales, in-game purchases, subscription services, sponsorship deals, ticket sales, media rights, and merchandise sales.

According to Statista.com:

  • The global esports market size is expected to grow from 1.28 billion US dollars to 2.89 billion US dollars. Representing a compound annual growth rate of 23 per cent
  • Professional esports team ‘Team Spirit’ earned almost 19 million US dollars in 2021
  • The highest-earning player in 2021 was Ukraine’s Miroslaw Kolpakov (Mira), who earned approximately 3.7 million US dollars
  • Founded in 2000, esports team ‘Team Liquid has earned almost 38 million US dollars in prize money
  • In 2021 Free Fire World Series hosted in Singapore became the most-watched esports event of all time with 5.41 million peak viewers
  • In 2021 esports tournament ‘The International’ has a total prize pool of over 40 million US dollars
  • The worldwide esports audience in 2021 was 474 million. Of this number 234 million fell in the category of esport enthusiasts
  • One of the leading esports organisations ‘Cloud9’ was reportedly worth 350 million US dollars in 2020

Developing this project is in line with my passion for gaming and investments. The project also has a massive potential to deliver a positive social impact, and improve the lives of young gamers around the world.

Desciption of Product

In order to achieve the vision for the project, it is important that deliberate thought and care be put into the research, design, development and launch of the esports crowdfunding platform. The platform will be a place where esports athletes can create a name for themself and showcase their achievements to potential investors. Athletes will sign up, be required to authenticate their email address, whilst agreeing to terms. They will then be directed to complete their profile much like any other social media platform.

Esports athlete profiles will include:

  • Full name
  • City and country of residence
  • Gamer name
  • Links to social and streaming profiles such as Twitch
  • Any teams they might belong to
  • Which games they play
  • How many hours they play on average per week
  • How many hours they have clocked on their favourite games
  • List of achievements such as how they have ranked in competitions
  • Previous prize money if any
  • Existing sponsorships and endorsement deals in any

Esports athletes will need to set a limit to the maximum percentage of sales they would like to make available on the platform for investors to purchase. The investment structure can take many forms. An example would be an esports athlete will make a total of 50 per cent of their total earnings available for purchase, as they might decide to keep the other 50 per cent to themselves. This can be purchased by one investor who would like to own 50 per cent of the earnings, or two investors own 25 per cent each, or five investors who own 10 per cent of the earnings each and so on. The esports athletes will need to be given general guidance on how to price their future earnings, but the choice will ultimately be theirs to make. This might include an algorithm that collates all the available data of the price of other esport athletes' earnings, and generates a range based on multiple variables on the data available. The data may be analysed on the basis of other comparable athletes, and ratios may be generated such as price per percentage of earnings, price per dollar of earnings, earnings average growth rate, and so on.

On the other side of the transaction will be investors. So the platform will have clear links to separate sections of the web app for investors and athletes. Investors will be given search functionality based on the profile questions that the athletes must input, as well as the variable investment parameters and investment ratios. This will help them pinpoint the search results to show the type of esport athletes they are seeking, and the type of investments they would like to engage in. An example of this is an investor might look to invest in esport athletes from a certain region of the world; they might seek a lower or higher level of risk; they might look for higher levels of growth, or esport athletes that present better ‘value for money’. he potential drawback is that there will be no simple solution to ensure that the information added to profiles is accurate. Investments are risky, and there are likely to be regulatory hurdles to cross depending on if the platform is deemed a financial product or service by regulatory bodies such as ASIC.

Technologies

The most likely format for the esports crowdfunding platform will be a web app. There is also potential later down the track for mobile apps to be developed for both iOS and Android platforms. As the product feature-set does not necessarily require functionality unique to smartphones, mobile apps may be dropped from the business model altogether allowing the engineering team to focus solely on the web app. The framework to be used for the web app will be made up of the MERN stack centred around Javascript. This is a popular development stack used by development teams at the moment with the MERN stack very prevalent amongst startups. The use of a common and popular set of development technologies will also make it easier down the track to hire and build an engineering team and developer talent in a cost-effective way.
